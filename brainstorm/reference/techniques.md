# Brainstorming techniques

Source: [Asana — Brainstorming techniques](https://asana.com/resources/brainstorming-techniques). Pick one to drive the generation phase (step 3 of SKILL.md); analytic ones double as convergence options (step 4).

**Scope note.** On the fast path, the picked technique drives the joint alternating phase. On the deep path, it scaffolds **the user's solo divergence only** — your side is scaffolded by the persona agents.

## Generative (use for volume / step 3)

- **Rapid Ideation** — generate as many ideas as possible quickly, without overthinking.
- **Brainwriting** — each side writes an idea, then builds on the other's next round.
- **Round-robin** — each side contributes at least one idea before either reacts.
- **Crazy Eights** — sketch/list eight ideas fast (or three in six minutes) — good for a quick volume burst.
- **Collaborative Brainwriting** — ideas added to a shared doc over time, built on cumulatively.
- **'What If' Brainstorming** — pose speculative scenarios ("what if X had no constraint Y?") and generate ideas off each one.
- **Wishing** — state a desired outcome, then generate pathways toward it.

## Reframing (use when stuck or need a fresh angle)

Domain-neutral — safe on either path, either side.

- **Reverse Brainstorming** — generate problems/failure modes first, then flip each into a solution.
- **Random Word Picker** — pull a random word, force a connection to the topic.
- **Change of Scenery** — reframe by imagining the problem in a wildly different context.

## Roleplay — **user's solo phase only**

Never assign these to your own side on the deep path. The persona agents (`deep-path.md` D2) already run this mechanic, in parallel and with a research-backed selection rule; running it on both sides double-counts personas and buys nothing.

Also note **Figure Storming's failure mode**, which the deep path's persona rule exists to avoid: notable/creative figures are the intuitive pick and the *worse* one. Ordinary personas measured 210 unique combinations vs. 164 for creative entrepreneurs ([source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20408)). If the user runs Figure Storming, steer them toward mundane figures over famous ones — and toward figures who are *inside the topic's situation*, since a mundane figure from an unrelated walk of life answers by analogy rather than from experience (`deep-path.md` D2 has the full rule).

- **Six Thinking Hats** — each turn takes a different lens: facts, emotion, caution, optimism, creativity, process.
- **Figure Storming** — "what would [famous/relevant person] do?" — answer as them.
- **Role Storming** — argue from the perspective of someone affected by the outcome.

## Analytic (use for step 5 convergence, or to structure one idea in step 3)

- **Starbursting** — six-point star of who/what/when/where/why/how around one idea.
- **Five Whys** — ask "why" five times to find root cause.
- **SWOT Analysis** — strengths / weaknesses / opportunities / threats.
- **How Now Wow** — plot ideas by originality × ease of implementation.
- **Drivers Analysis** — repeatedly ask what drives the problem.
- **Mind Mapping** — central topic with radiating branches.
- **Gap Analysis** — map current state → desired state, list obstacles.
- **Lightning Decision Jam** — positives/negatives/solutions into a priority matrix, ~40–60 min.

## Structural (for bigger or multi-idea sessions)

- **Idea Napkin** — develop one idea with pitch, audience, problem solved.
- **Storyboarding** — visual sequence of problem → solution.
- **Charrette** — split a big problem into parts, rotate through each.
- **Step-ladder** — add participants one at a time before revealing prior ideas. Note: the deep path is a stronger version of this — fully parallel rather than staggered, so no participant is ever anchored by an earlier one.
- **Eidetic Image Method** — visualize the concept, then imagine enhancements in cycles.
- **Reverse Thinking** — compare an alternative persona's approach against your own. *User's solo phase only* — same reason as the Roleplay section.
